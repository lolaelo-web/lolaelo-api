Big & Small Lagoon

Big & Small Lagoon

These are the signature visuals of El Nido. They are also the most timing-sensitive stops in Bacuit Bay.

Browse stays Jump to Top 10 Back to guide

What it is

A dramatic limestone lagoon with calm water, usually reached by kayak. When timed right, it is genuinely memorable.

What it is not

A quiet, empty escape. Midday crowds and queueing can flatten the experience fast.

The plan that works

  • Go early. This matters more here than anywhere else.
  • Enter, paddle, and exit before the bulk of boats arrive.
  • Spend your time in the water, not waiting on the platform.

Bring

  • Dry bag
  • Water shoes
  • Sun protection

Nice to have

  • Kayak gloves
  • Snorkel mask
  • Light towel

Skip

  • Bulky backpacks
  • Expectations of solitude
  • Late arrival plans

Mistakes that ruin this stop

Showing up late, rushing through for photos, and stacking this with too many other stops in one day. This is a timing play, not a checklist item.

Early entry changes everything

The same lagoon feels completely different at 8am versus noon.

Less time, better memory

You do not need long here. You need the right window.

FAQ

Big Lagoon or Small Lagoon?

If you only do one, Big Lagoon. If timing is good, doing both back to back works.

Is kayaking required?

Usually yes. That is part of the experience and part of the bottleneck.

Is this good for kids?

Yes, if conditions are calm and you go early. Avoid peak hours.
