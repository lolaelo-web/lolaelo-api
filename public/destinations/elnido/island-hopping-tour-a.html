Island hopping Tour A

Island hopping Tour A

Tour A can be the best day of your trip or a crowded blur. The difference is your start time, your expectations, and how tight you keep the plan.

What to expect

Tour A is lagoon-heavy and photo-heavy. It is also the one that can feel the most crowded. Your goal is simple: show up early, get your best stops first, and protect actual swim time.

Duration: full day Effort: medium Crowds: high Best start: early

A 3-step plan that works

1

Lock the morning

Be ready early and treat the first 2 hours as the priority window. That is when the water is calmer and crowds are lower.

2

Pick fewer stops, stay longer

Do not rush every location. Choose the best few and spend enough time to actually swim and reset.

3

End clean

Save one calmer stop for the end. If the day is chaotic, finishing with a quieter stretch keeps the trip from feeling like a checklist.

Bring

  • Dry bag (phone, wallet, towel)
  • Reef-safe sunscreen + hat
  • Water shoes if you hate sharp footing
  • Simple snacks and hydration

Avoid

  • Overpacking. You will carry it all day.
  • Assuming every stop is quiet. It won’t be.
  • Late starts. You lose the best window.

If weather shifts

  • Switch to a beach day (Nacpan or Lio) and keep the boat day for the first good window.
  • Do not force it if sea conditions are rough. It is not worth it.

Lagoon time is the whole point

Do not spend your best hours waiting around. Move early, get the lagoons first, and give yourself real time in the water.

Protect one slow hour

Even on a full-day tour, you can build a calm pocket. That one slow stretch is what makes the day feel like a vacation.

FAQ

Is Tour A worth it if I hate crowds?

It can be, but only if you start early and keep the pace tight. If you want a cleaner vibe, Tour C is usually the better bet.

What is the single best way to improve this day?

Start early. That is it. It changes everything.

Can I do Tour A and Tour C back to back?

You can, but it is a lot. Better: separate them with a beach day so you actually recover.

What if I only have 3 days in El Nido?

Do Tour A, take one real beach day, and keep one evening for a clean sunset at Las Cabanas. That is enough.
