Pick your lane

Choose the version that matches your body and your day. The hot spring is a tool. Use it like one.

Quick reset

45 to 60 min total

For: you want the benefit without the fatigue.

  • 1 short soak cycle
  • Hydrate before and after
  • Leave while you still feel fresh

Full recovery

75 to 110 min total

For: post-boat day soreness or a heavy itinerary.

  • 2 controlled soak cycles
  • Cool down between cycles
  • Light dinner after, then sleep

Family mode

30 to 60 min total

For: kids or anyone who overheats fast.

  • Shorter cycles, more breaks
  • Keep water time conservative
  • Hydration is the priority

The Maquinit protocol

  1. Hydrate first. Water before you enter. You are not negotiating this.
  2. Cycle, don’t bake. 10 to 15 minutes in, then 5 minutes out.
  3. Listen for overheating. If you feel even slightly off, you stop.
  4. Exit clean. Leave while you still feel good, not when you feel drained.
  5. Finish with simple food. Light dinner, then sleep.

Short sessions beat long sessions

People ruin this by treating it like a challenge. The benefit comes from controlled heat exposure, not suffering.

Win: you leave feeling lighter
Loss: you leave dehydrated

Best timing is late afternoon

If you go earlier, you stack heat on top of heat. Late afternoon is when it turns into recovery instead of punishment.

Best play: post-boat day
Skip: midday as a main event

FAQ

When is the best time to go?

Late afternoon to early evening. It works best as a recovery reset after a boat day.

How long should I stay?

Keep water time to 30 to 60 minutes. The goal is recovery, not endurance.

What is the biggest mistake?

Staying too long and dehydrating. Leave while you still feel good.

Is it kid friendly?

Yes with supervision. Keep sessions shorter and prioritize hydration.
