Reality check

Wrecks are not “just another dive.” There are overhead risks, depth considerations, and visibility variables. Your best dive is the one you finish comfortably.

Pick your lane

Choose based on training and comfort. Not vibes.

Certified: conservative wreck tour

Best default

For: certified divers who want the experience without pushing limits.

  • No penetration unless trained
  • Stay with guide and group plan
  • Prioritize buoyancy and calm pace

Advanced: depth + complexity

Only if ready

For: experienced divers with recent dives and comfort at depth.

  • Be honest about recent dive history
  • Plan gas conservatively
  • Abort early if anything feels off

Not certified: discover programs

Strict rules

For: beginners under a formal program with instructor control.

  • Stay shallow and supervised
  • No penetration, no independence
  • If you feel fear, you stop

Safety-first protocol

  1. Be honest at the shop. Certification, recent dives, comfort, equalize issues.
  2. Confirm the plan. Depth, time, current, wreck type, and whether penetration is involved.
  3. Conservative gas planning. Leave margin. Do not chase “one more look.”
  4. Stay with the guide. Group discipline is a safety tool.
  5. Abort early if needed. Pride is not part of the dive plan.

Hard rules

  • No penetration unless trained and planned
  • No solo decisions
  • Abort if gear issues appear
  • Respect depth and no-deco limits

Comfort rules

  • If anxiety rises, stop and signal
  • Slow descent, slow ascent
  • Warm up with an easier dive if needed
  • Leave before fatigue hits

FAQ

Can beginners do Coron wreck diving?

Some operators offer discover-style experiences, but wreck environments are serious. Follow program rules and do not attempt penetration.

How do I choose the right wreck day?

Choose based on certification, comfort, and recent dive history. Conservative plans are the best plans.

What is the biggest mistake?

Trying dives that exceed training or comfort. Wrecks are not the place to improvise.

Do I need special gear?

Depends on depth and conditions. Follow the dive shop’s guidance and stay within your training.
