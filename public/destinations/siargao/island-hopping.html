Route that works

Three stops. No extra detours. This keeps the day smooth, especially if you are with family or a mixed group.

1

Guyam: quick swim + photos

Small, clean, fast. Do not camp here for hours. Use it as the “warm-up” stop.

2

Daku: food + shade break

This is where you slow down. Eat, rehydrate, let kids decompress. This is the anchor stop.

3

Naked: quick sandbar hit

Short, fun, clean. It is a sandbar, not a full beach day. Get in, get the moment, get out.

Bring

  • Dry bag (or at least a sealed pouch for phone + cash)
  • Water + snacks
  • Sun protection (hat or long sleeve)
  • Cash in small bills

Avoid

  • Overpacking valuables
  • Stacking “extra islands” just to say you did more
  • Late starts that turn into heat + crowd management
  • No shade plan if you have kids

Guyam: keep it quick

Guyam is the clean opener. Swim, take the shot, get your bearings. Do not burn the whole morning here. You want momentum.

Best use: warm-up stop
Skip: “long hang”

Daku: the real break

This is where you eat and chill. If the day starts to feel chaotic, fix it here. Hydrate, shade, and a slower pace resets the whole group.

Do: food + shade
Do: reapply sunblock

Naked: treat it like a moment

It is a sandbar. The magic is that it is simple. Go when the light is good, enjoy it, then move on before it becomes uncomfortable.

Best: quick swim
Watch: sun exposure

FAQ

How long is the island hopping trip?

Plan on a half day. The water time is usually a few hours, plus some buffer for meet-up and return.

Can we do this with kids?

Yes. The key is shade breaks, water, and not pushing the pace. Make Daku your long stop.

Is there a “best order” for the islands?

Guyam first, Daku second, Naked last works well. It keeps the day structured and prevents decision fatigue.

What ruins the experience?

Late starts, overpacking, and stacking extra stops. Keep it simple and the day stays clean.
