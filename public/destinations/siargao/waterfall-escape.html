What to expect

This is a short stop. The win is a cool-down and a change of scenery. If you expect a massive hike or epic canyon day, you will be disappointed.

  • Good for a mid-trip reset.
  • Best when you keep it quick.
  • Pair it with an easy road stop and lunch.

Quick plan that works

Go midday, cool off, take a few photos, then leave while it still feels clean.

  • Bring water and small cash.
  • Do 45 to 90 minutes on site.
  • Exit and go eat nearby.

Use it as a cool-down

The best way to enjoy this is simple: show up, cool off, sit for a few minutes, then leave. If you try to stretch it into a big “activity,” it drags.

Best: quick reset
Skip: overplanning

Make it part of a road loop

Pair it with one or two simple stops on the way back. Keep the day light, not stacked. That is how you avoid “day trip fatigue.”

Pair: easy stops
Result: smooth day

FAQ

Is this worth doing if I only have 3 days?

Only if you want a change of scene. If your time is tight, prioritize surfing, Magpupungko, and one big water day first.

How long should we stay?

1 to 3 hours total is enough. Most people are done in 45 to 90 minutes on site.

What should we bring?

Water, small cash, and something you do not mind getting wet. Keep valuables minimal.

What is the biggest mistake?

Overhyping it. Treat it as a reset and it will feel worth it.
