How to use this guide

Siargao is easy to overbook. This list keeps you focused. If you only have 3 days, prioritize: Surfing lessons, Magpupungko, and one easy island or lagoon day.

Best trip length: 3 to 5 days Style: surf, lagoons, island days Effort: low to medium

Quick picks

  • First time: #1 Surfing lessons, #3 Sugba Lagoon, #4 Magpupungko
  • Families: #2 Island hopping, #5 Coconut road, #9 Wellness reset
  • Active: #1 Surfing, #4 Rock pools, #8 Waterfall escape
  • Chill: #5 Coconut road, #7 Sunset ritual, repeat beach resets

Timing that matters

  • Tide days: Magpupungko is tide-dependent, plan it first
  • Day trips: leave early for Sugba and island hopping
  • Surf sessions: mornings are often cleaner, ask instructors
  • Night rides: avoid risky late bike driving

FAQ

How many days do you need in Siargao?

Three to five days is the sweet spot. Three days for surfing plus one day trip, five days if you want to slow down and stack less per day.

Do you need a motorbike?

It helps, but it is not required. You can use tricycles, vans, and tours. If you ride, avoid risky night driving.

What should you plan first?

Plan tide-dependent Magpupungko first, then lock in one early-start day trip (Sugba or island hopping). Everything else can stay flexible.

Is Siargao good for families?

Yes. Just plan earlier starts for trips and keep a low-effort day between excursions. Add sunset sessions and beach resets instead of stacking intense days.
