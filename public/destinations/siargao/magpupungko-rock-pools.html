What it is

A rock pool window that feels like a natural swimming spot. When conditions are right, it is one of the coolest “only here” moments in Siargao.

What it is not

Not a guaranteed swim at any time. If you show up without checking tide, you are gambling on the whole trip.

Plan that works

Treat it like a tide mission. Your goal is to arrive inside the window, enjoy it, then exit before it turns.

  • Pick your day based on tide and weather, not vibes.
  • Arrive earlier than you think, so you are not rushing.
  • Wear the right footwear, move slow, and stay in calmer areas.

Mistakes that ruin it

1) Going at the wrong tide. 2) Wearing the wrong footwear. 3) Treating slippery rock like normal pavement. Slow down. Stay in safe zones. This is not the day for hero moves.

Timing is the whole game

When it is right, the pools feel calm and clear. When it is wrong, it is rough and less safe. Build the day around the tide window, not the other way around.

Do: plan around tide
Result: perfect swim

Make it a clean road trip

Treat the drive as part of the day. Bring water, keep the car simple, and do not stack extra detours. One mission done well beats five stops done sloppy.

Keep it: simple
Skip: overstacking

FAQ

Do I need to check tide before going?

Yes. Tide is the whole point. If you do not check it, you are rolling the dice on the experience.

What shoes should I wear?

Water shoes with grip. Flip flops and bare feet are how people slip and ruin the day.

Can we go with kids?

Yes, but stay close, pick calmer pool zones, and move slower than you think you need to.

How long should we stay?

Long enough to enjoy the tide window. For most people, 2 to 4 hours including time on site is plenty.
