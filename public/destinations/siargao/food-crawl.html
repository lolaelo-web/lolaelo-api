3-stop route (works every time)

Do not turn this into a 9-stop mission. Three stops is perfect. You remember the night, not the spreadsheet.

1

Starter: something light

Appetizer or small plate. The goal is to start the night without filling up immediately.

2

Main: the real dinner stop

This is the anchor. Sit, eat, hydrate. Make this the one place you do not rush.

3

Finish: dessert or one drink

End the night simply. Dessert is a clean win. If you drink, keep it controlled and call it early.

Order strategy

Do not have everyone order the same thing. Split the table into lanes: 1) something local 2) something fresh 3) one safe comfort option.

  • Share plates = more variety.
  • Drink water between stops.
  • Stop when you are satisfied, not destroyed.

Rain plan

If it starts raining, your strategy is simple: stay longer at the main stop, then finish at one nearby place. Do not bounce around in the rain just to “keep the route.”

  • Main stop becomes the anchor.
  • Finish stop should be close.
  • Call it early and still win.

Pick one “real” dinner spot

This is the night’s anchor. If you get this right, the whole crawl feels easy. Pick somewhere you can sit, talk, and actually enjoy the food.

Do: sit and slow down
Skip: rushing plates

End before it turns

The best food crawl ends clean. If you push too late, it stops being fun and starts being sloppy. Eat, walk, finish, done.

FAQ

How many stops is ideal?

Three. Starter, main, finish. More than that usually becomes chaos.

What if we cannot decide where to go?

Pick the main stop first. Everything else can be nearby. The main stop is the only real decision.

Is this good for families?

Yes, if you start earlier and keep it to two stops. Kids do better with one main stop and one simple finish.

What is the biggest mistake?

Trying to do too much. Three stops is plenty. Keep it clean and you will actually enjoy it.
