Pick your lane

Choose a style based on your comfort level. This keeps the day smooth.

Lane A: First-timers
Snorkel only. Stay close to shore. Focus on confidence and calm breathing.
Lane B: Comfortable swimmers
Snorkel plus a short reef loop. Add an easy beach break.
Lane C: Strong swimmers
Longer water time, optional freedive dips. Keep your spacing. Avoid fatigue ego.

Safety basics (keep it non-negotiable)

Buddy rule

No solo sessions. One buddy stays close enough to actually help.

Breathing first

If you are breathless, you are done. Exit, reset, and decide calmly.

Gear fit

Mask seal and fins fit matter more than photos. Fix it before you enter.

FAQ

Do I need a boat to see the sardines?

No. The main sardine run experience is commonly accessible from shore. Conditions and exact spots can shift, so keep expectations realistic.

Is this okay for non-swimmers?

Not ideal. If you are not comfortable in water, skip the deep zone and choose a calmer coastal plan. Do not force it.

What is the best time of day?

Morning. Better light, calmer water, and less crowd stress.

What should we pair this with?

Keep it light. A relaxed lunch and an easy coast stop. Do not overpack the day just because you "made the drive."
